Legal Fees Explained for Calgary & Alberta Real Estate

What you really pay, what's included, and what to watch for

What a Real Estate Lawyer Actually Does

A real estate lawyer's role is not just "paperwork." They are legally responsible for critical parts of your transaction that carry real liability.

Reviewing and explaining the real estate contract
Ensuring clear title to the property
Registering ownership and mortgage documents
Handling and accounting for trust funds
Managing lender instructions and conditions
Calculating financial adjustments
Ensuring legal possession is properly transferred

Liability matters: If something goes wrong, the lawyer ‚Äî not the realtor ‚Äî carries legal liability for these functions.

Legal Fees vs Disbursements: Understanding the Distinction

This is where confusion begins. Understanding this distinction is crucial to avoiding billing surprises.

Legal Fees
What the lawyer charges for their professional work. Often offered as a flat fee (most common) or occasionally hourly (rare for standard transactions).
Disbursements
Out-of-pocket costs the lawyer pays on your behalf. Examples include land title registration fees, title searches, courier and software fees, bank processing fees, condo document handling, and title insurance.

Common misunderstanding: Many people think disbursements are "extra padding." They are not ‚Äî but they are often underestimated or poorly explained.

What "Flat-Rate Legal Fees" Really Mean

Many Calgary law firms advertise flat-rate real estate fees. This is helpful ‚Äî but only if you understand the scope of what's included.

A flat rate usually assumes:
  • One buyer and one seller
  • One title
  • One mortgage
  • No unusual conditions or defects
  • Standard closing timeline
The flat rate often does NOT include:
  • Multiple mortgages
  • Private or secondary lenders
  • Title issues or encroachments
  • Power of attorney situations
  • Trust conditions requiring extra work
  • Additional properties or parking titles (condos)

Important distinction: Flat fee ‚â† capped total cost in every situation. Complexities can trigger additional legal fees.

Why Legal Fees Increase in "Non-Standard" Deals

Some situations require more legal work and carry more risk, such as:

Condo purchases Multiple documents, reserve fund review
Estate sales Probate, executor authority
Rentals with tenants Tenancy agreements, notices
Private mortgages Alternative lender requirements

Understanding higher costs: In these cases, higher fees usually reflect real work and real liability, not padding. More complex transactions require more review, more documentation, and carry higher professional risk.

Buying vs Selling: Key Fee Differences

Buyers Pay More Overall

Buyers typically pay more because lawyers must:

  • Review lender instructions carefully
  • Register the mortgage on title
  • Coordinate funds from multiple sources
  • Verify title and tax adjustments
  • Handle more conditions overall

Sellers Pay Slightly Less

Sellers typically pay less because:

  • No mortgage is being registered
  • Fewer lender instructions to manage
  • Fewer conditions overall
  • Less coordination of incoming funds
  • Generally simpler title work

Setting expectations: Understanding this natural cost difference helps set fair expectations when comparing quotes or discussing fees with your lawyer.

The Most Common Surprises Clients Face

"I thought that was included in the flat fee"
"No one mentioned extra title costs for the condo"
"Why are there bank fees from the lender?"

The root cause: Almost always, these surprises come from not reviewing the fee letter carefully or not asking enough questions about what's included and what's not.

Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Real Estate Lawyer

Before you sign, ask:

  • What exactly is included in the flat fee?
  • What situations increase the cost?
  • Are disbursements estimated or capped?
  • How are condo titles handled?
  • What lender fees are separate from legal fees?
  • How do you handle title issues if they arise?
  • What's your process for explaining adjustments?
  • How do you communicate during the transaction?

Clear answers now prevent frustration later.
